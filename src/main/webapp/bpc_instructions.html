Business Policy Concession (BPC)

A BPC budget is available so that Eaton Electrical can be responsive to customer needs and disputes over controversial issues. If the issue is not a field sales error or a product performance related issue, field sales can initiate a BPC claim.

BPC provides managers with an excellent tool to "do the right thing" commercially on behalf of Eaton.

I. Purpose of BPC

The Zone BPC account is intended to be used as a vehicle to
make a commercial concession to a valued customer that is above and beyond Eaton’s normal contractual responsibility.

Field sales errors should be covered by using the local field sales concession account (Z-account).

Product Lines should normally be the source of total or partial funds for a concession when product line quality is the root cause of the problem on which the claim is based.

BPC is NOT intended to be used to cover contractual warranty
expenses that are clearly the responsibility of a product line.

||. Other considerations:

  1. BPC can be used to authorize up to one man-day of Eaton Electrical Sales& Services (E-ESS). The field sales organization should set up the scheduling with their local E-ESS group and then, contact the BPC Manager for order entry and a BPC reference number.
  2. The determination of who shall pay for this service is dependent upon E-ESS findings. The customer must understand that if the results of the investigation demonstrate that it is not a Eaton responsibility, the customer will be invoiced for performed services.

In the event that it is determined to be a Eaton responsibility, the appropriate product line will be expected to absorb the charges.

III. Procedure

  1. A BPC claim must be initiated by the sales organization. Any incomplete forms will be returned to the requester for more detail therefore delaying the processing of the request.
  2. The forms must be forwarded from the e-mail box for the appropriate location.
  3. Upon appropriate approval, the BPC Manager is responsible for initiating the expenditure transaction via established procedures.
