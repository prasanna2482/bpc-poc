BUSINESS POLICY CONCESSION (BPC)

Detailed Instructions

A BPC budget is available so that Cutler Hammer can be responsive to customer needs and disputes over controversial issues. If the issue is not a field sales error or a product performance related issue, a decision can be made by field sales District Managers and Zone Managers to be charged to the BPC. District Managers and Zone Managers have authorization up to the amount of $1,000 and $5,000, respectively.

BPC provides managers with an excellent tool to "do the right thing" commercially on behalf of Cutler-Hammer.

I. Purpose of BPC


The Zone BPC account is intended to be used as a vehicle to
make a commercial concession to a valued customer that is above and beyond Cutler-Hammer's normal contractual responsibility.

Field sales errors should be covered by using the local field sales concession account (Z-account).

Product Lines should normally be the source of total or partial funds for a concession when that division's good will is important.

BPC is not intended to be used to cover contractual warranty
expenses that are clearly the responsibility of a product line.


II. Limits of Authority


A. District Sales Manager -- Up to $1,000.

B. Zone and National Sales Manager -- Up to $5,000.

C. Zone BPC Manager -- Above $5,000.

D. Other considerations:

a. BPC can be used to authorize up to one man-day of Cutler-Hammer Engineering Service (CH-ESS) even though the total charges may exceed the $1,000 authorization limit of the District Manager. The field sales organization should set up the scheduling with their local CH-ESS group and then, contact the BPC Manager for order entry and a BPC reference number.

b. The determination of who shall pay for this service is dependent upon CH-ESS findings. The customer must understand that if the results of the investigation demonstrate that it is not a Cutler-Hammer responsibility, the customer will be invoiced for performed services.

In the event that it is determined to be a Cutler-Hammer responsibility, the appropriate product line will be expected to absorb the charges.

III. Procedure

1) A BPC form must be initiated by the sales organization. Any incompleted forms will be returned to the requester for more detail therefore delaying the processing of the request.

2) The forms must be forwarded from the e-mail box of the authorizing manager.

a. When the concession is under $5,000 the completed form must be forwarded to the BPC manager no later than seven days after commitment to the customer.

b. If the concession is over $5,000, the BPC manager must be included in the process before any final commitment is made to the customer.

1) Upon appropriate approval, Zone BPC Manager is responsible for initiating the expenditure transaction via established procedures.

IV. BPC Form Instructions


A. Attached to this Web Site is a blank BPC form.

1. Transmittal via email is preferred.
2. Hard copy mailing for fax is acceptable.

B. Please be thorough. Besides being used for an approval process, the information is entered into a data base used for disposition of those charges to the appropriate product lines as determined based upon the details you provide on the BPC approval form.

C. Form Completion instructions (Refer to attached BPC form)

1. From The district manager's office.
2. Adnet The district manager's Adnet number.
3. Date Of completion of form.
4. Subject Leave as is.
5. To Leave as is.
6. cc The manager that the district sales manager reports to should always be sent a copy, regardless of amount.
7. G.O. NO. The General Order and item number of the ORIGINAL product or service that is causing the concession to be made.
8. Salesman The salesman following this problem and/or responsible for this customer.
9. Division The product line supplying the product or service at issue.
10. Product The product at issue.
11. Distributor If sold through distributor.
12. Contractor If involved in the sale to the ultimate user (could also be an O.E.M.).
13. User The end user.
14. Location(s) City and state.
15. Total Amount of Claim The total the customer requested.
16. Settlement The total settlement to the customer, including division contribution, as well as BPC.
17. Settlement By Check the method used to expend funds out of the BPC account.
18. Detail Reason for Concession Why are we making the concession, i.e., what is causing the need for the concession?
19. Root Cause Root cause codes will be determined by the Zone BPC Manager.
20. Authorized Signature & BPC Limit a. If transmitting via email, typed name is sufficient if authorizing manager is sending it from his own mailbox.
b. Transmittal by email for approval greater than $1,000 can be timesaving, as the district manager can forward to the region manager, who in turn can forward to the BPC manager, cc back to the district manager with a simple statement of "I approve."
